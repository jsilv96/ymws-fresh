Cookie Policy

What cookies we use and how to manage your preferences.

Home / Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 1, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how Flame & Fork uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website. It also describes how you can control and adjust your preferences.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the site remember your preferences, keep you signed in, and understand how visitors interact with the site. Similar technologies like pixels and local storage work in comparable ways.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Category Purpose Example
Essential Required for the site to function — logging in, cart, checkout. session_id, cart_token
Preferences Remember your location, language, and cookie consent choices. rm_cookie_consent, locale
Analytics Help us understand traffic, popular pages, and how people use the site. Google Analytics (_ga)
Marketing Serve relevant promotions and measure ad performance. Meta Pixel, Google Ads

3. Manage Your Preferences

Use the controls below to set your cookie preferences. Essential cookies cannot be disabled because the site will not function without them.

Essential Cookies

Necessary for core site features. Always on.

Preference Cookies

Remember your choices like language and location.

Analytics Cookies

Help us understand how you use our website.

Marketing Cookies

Serve personalized promotions across other websites.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some of the cookies we use are placed by third parties, such as analytics and advertising partners. These parties have their own privacy policies that govern how they handle the data they collect.

5. Browser Controls

You can also manage cookies directly in your browser settings. Most browsers let you block or delete cookies. Keep in mind that disabling essential cookies may affect how the site works. Instructions are available for:

  • Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge — check your browser's Help menu.
  • Mobile browsers — settings available in the browser app.

6. Do Not Track

Some browsers transmit a "Do Not Track" signal. Because there is no industry standard for how to respond to these signals, we currently do not alter our practices based on them. You can still manage your preferences using the controls above.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. When we do, we'll post the new version on this page and update the "Last updated" date above.
